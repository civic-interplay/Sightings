## Design Context

### Users
Two overlapping audiences, workshop-first:
- **Primary — workshop participants in a room.** Community members, researchers, and artists gathered in a physical space for a facilitated Civic Interplay session. The site runs on a projector as an ambient backdrop while people mingle, notice, and submit what they see/hear/feel.
- **Secondary — public / online visitors.** Someone who lands on sightings.civicinterplay.io on a laptop without a facilitator. It must hold up as a contemplative invitation on its own.

The job to be done: make "collective noticing" feel *alive* — so the room sees the tapestry of what's been shared, and submitting a noticing feels like planting something real into a shared field rather than filling out a form.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: strange, more-than-human, noticing.**

- Not minimal-Swiss, not cozy-community, not tech-product. The register is a naturalist's field journal crossed with a weather station ledger crossed with a slightly uncanny dream.
- Hushed and attentive — whispering rather than announcing. Rewards patient looking.
- Surfaces what's usually invisible (ecological, social, sensory, cultural, hydrological intelligences). The mood is "field-notes-from-elsewhere," not dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** dark. The piece is viewed in dim workshop rooms and lives better against deep, tinted darks than bright surfaces. Garden view has a light mode for contrast; Tapestry and the WebGL / room view stay dark.
- **Inherited palette (do not reinvent):** pulled from civicinterplay.io —
  - Ecological `#454E41` (olive)
  - Social `#D16D54` (terracotta)
  - Sensory `#E076DB` (pink)
  - Cultural `#7D50BD` (purple)
  - Water `#8E9BDD` (periwinkle)
  - Supporting: `#DACCBA` (sand) · `#F8DFF6` (lavender) · `#000000` ground
- **Typography pairing:** Young Serif (display) · Alegreya 400/400i/500 (body, with italic for sighting quotes) · Fragment Mono 400/500 (labels, metadata, counters). All outside the reflex-font list. Mono is earned here — it's the weather-station / field-log register, not a developer trope.
- **Anti-references:** do not look like an AI-art landing page (dark with neon-gradient accents), do not look like a crypto dashboard (glassy cards with cyan glow), do not look like a Notion/Linear app (boxy cards with left-stripe accents, drop shadows, rounded rectangles in a grid). No gradient text. No `border-left` stripes as kind indicators.

### Design Principles
1. **The room sees this first, the browser second.** Type must be legible from across a workshop space. Avoid tiny labels that disappear at projector resolution. Chrome collapses into projection mode cleanly.
2. **The shader is the piece.** Backgrounds are not decoration; they are the primary surface. Everything else (cards, controls) is pinned *to* the shader, not layered *over* it. Cards should breathe with the background, not sit on top of it.
3. **Strange over safe.** When a choice is between "polished and templated" and "a little uncanny," choose uncanny. The aesthetic reward of this project is the feeling of recognizing something usually invisible.
4. **Inherited palette, fresh deployment.** Do not invent new hex values. Change *how* the existing palette is used — translucency, tinted neutrals, specimen-dots instead of color-block rectangles.
5. **Motion is slow and tidal.** This is a projection running for hours. Every animation should be watchable for 10 minutes without fatigue. Easing is exponential; durations are long (seconds, not milliseconds) for ambient layers.
